How to Make Your Job Application Stand Out

Employers often receive hundreds of applications, so making yours compelling, professional, and relevant increases your chances of landing an interview.

1. Include a Cover Letter (Even if Optional)
A cover letter highlights your personality, enthusiasm, and qualifications.
  • Keep it between 250 and 400 words.
  • Tailor it specifically to the role.
  • Use examples to demonstrate why you’re a great fit.
2. Keep Your Resume Concise and Clear
Hiring managers scan resumes quickly, so make yours easy to read:
  • Use a one-page resume for early-career professionals.
  • Stick to two pages max for experienced candidates.
  • Use bullet points and clear headings for readability.
3. Highlight What Makes You Unique
Identify and emphasize:
  • Key strengths, achievements, and experiences.
  • Unique skills, certifications, or industry expertise.
4. Tailor Your Application for Each Job
Customize your resume and cover letter to fit the specific job description and company culture. Research the company and align your skills accordingly.
5. Showcase Your Most Relevant Skills and Experience
Use a reverse-chronological format and include:
  • Action-oriented bullet points with measurable results.
  • Specific metrics, e.g., "Increased sales by 20%."
  • A mix of hard skills (e.g., coding) and soft skills (e.g., leadership).
6. Proofread to Eliminate Mistakes
Avoid grammar and spelling errors:
  • Use spell-check tools.
  • Have someone review your application.
  • Read your resume out loud to catch mistakes.
7. Use Keywords to Pass Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS)
Many employers use ATS software to filter resumes. Improve your chances by:
  • Using keywords from the job description.
  • Keeping standard section headings (e.g., "Work Experience").
  • Avoiding excessive formatting (tables, graphics, or unusual fonts).
8. Demonstrate Enthusiasm and Cultural Fit
Show genuine excitement for the role and company:
  • Mention specific aspects of the company that resonate with you.
  • Highlight experiences that align with their culture.
  • Explain why you’d be a great fit.
9. Follow All Application Instructions Carefully
Many candidates are disqualified for not following instructions. Pay attention to:
  • File format requirements (e.g., "Submit as PDF").
  • Requested work samples or additional documents.
  • Submission deadlines and application portals.

Final Thoughts

By applying these strategies, you can create a job application that is clear, compelling, and tailored to the role. Employers appreciate candidates who take the time to present themselves professionally and thoughtfully. A well-structured resume and a personalized cover letter can significantly increase your chances of getting noticed and securing an interview.
