Position Title: Human Resources Manager

Brief Description: Responsible for overseeing all HR functions within the organization, including recruitment, employee relations, performance management, training, and compliance with labor laws. Ensures that the company's HR policies align with business goals and contribute to a positive organizational culture.

Salary Range: $80,000 - $110,000 per year

Reports To: Director of Human Resources

Key Responsibilities
  • Manage the recruitment and selection process, including job postings, interviewing, and hiring
  • Develop and implement HR policies and procedures to ensure compliance with labor laws and best practices
  • Oversee employee relations, resolving conflicts and addressing employee concerns in a timely manner
  • Manage performance appraisal processes, including goal setting, reviews, and employee feedback
  • Ensure employee training and development programs are aligned with organizational needs and individual growth
  • Administer compensation and benefits programs, including payroll, insurance, and retirement plans
  • Maintain employee records and HR databases, ensuring accuracy and confidentiality
  • Advise management on HR issues, including employee engagement, legal compliance, and organizational development
  • Foster a positive organizational culture and work environment that promotes employee retention

Qualifications

Essential:

  • Bachelor's degree in Human Resources, Business Administration, or a related field
  • At least 5 years of experience in human resources, with a focus on employee relations, recruitment, and compliance
  • In-depth knowledge of labor laws, regulations, and HR best practices
  • Strong communication, interpersonal, and leadership skills
  • Experience with HRIS systems and Microsoft Office Suite
  • Ability to manage multiple projects and meet deadlines in a fast-paced environment

Preferable:

  • Master's degree in Human Resources, Business Administration, or a related field
  • Professional certifications such as SHRM-CP, PHR, or SPHR
  • Experience with performance management systems and talent development
  • Strong conflict resolution skills and ability to manage sensitive situations
  • Experience with labor relations and union negotiations (if applicable)
